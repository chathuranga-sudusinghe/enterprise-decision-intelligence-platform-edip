# Post-Promotion Inventory Recovery Guide
## NorthStar Retail & Distribution (NRD)
## EDIP / Phase 5 RAG Knowledge Layer

## Version: 1.0
## Effective Date: 2025-01-01

## Approved By: Director, Commercial Planning and Inventory Recovery

## 1. Purpose

This guide defines the standard response approach for post-promotion inventory recovery situations at NorthStar Retail & Distribution (NRD).

Its purpose is to ensure that inventory remaining after a promotion is reviewed, prioritized, and recovered in a controlled, business-focused way before residual stock creates avoidable overstock, markdown pressure, warehouse congestion, inventory aging, or repeated commercial inefficiency.

This guide supports the Enterprise Decision Intelligence Platform (EDIP) by providing governed knowledge for:

- post-promotion inventory recovery
- residual stock assessment
- replenishment and inbound adjustment
- transfer and markdown support decisions
- commercial recovery coordination
- escalation and approval support
- audit-aware decision traceability

---

## 2. Scope

This guide applies to post-promotion inventory recovery situations involving:

- residual stock left after campaign end
- slower-than-expected sell-through after promotion close
- campaign stock remaining above recovery threshold
- excess inventory concentrated in selected stores or regions
- warehouse residual stock after event-driven positioning
- future inbound still planned after campaign demand ends
- markdown or secondary promotion consideration
- transfer-based stock rebalancing after campaign close
- repeated post-promotion residual patterns on similar campaigns
- campaign inventory positions that materially weaken inventory productivity or warehouse flow

This guide applies to teams such as:

- commercial planning
- merchandising
- pricing teams
- supply chain planning
- demand planning
- warehouse operations
- regional operations
- store operations
- inventory control
- finance support where inventory value exposure is relevant
- executive stakeholders for major recovery-risk cases

---

## 3. Definition of Post-Promotion Inventory Recovery

A post-promotion inventory recovery case exists when inventory positioned for a completed or closing promotion remains above the level needed for healthy post-event demand and therefore requires controlled corrective action.

Examples include:

- campaign ends with materially higher residual stock than planned
- sell-through slows sharply after promotional demand window closes
- stores retain excess campaign stock with weak recovery outlook
- warehouse still holds campaign-positioned inventory after event completion
- future inbound is still scheduled even though campaign demand has passed
- repeated promotions on similar items leave recurring residual stock exposure

A post-promotion inventory recovery case is not only a stock clean-up activity.  
It is a commercial, inventory, and working-capital control process.

---

## 4. Core Principles

All post-promotion inventory recovery responses must follow these principles.

### 4.1 Start recovery early
Recovery planning should begin before campaign close when residual stock risk is already visible.

### 4.2 Focus on business impact
Response actions must consider working capital, markdown exposure, warehouse pressure, inventory aging, and realistic sell-through potential.

### 4.3 Separate normal residual stock from abnormal overhang
Not all remaining inventory is a problem. Teams should focus on stock positions that are materially above healthy post-promotion need.

### 4.4 Protect inventory flow
Recovery should reduce excess efficiently without creating unnecessary disruption to healthier inventory flow.

### 4.5 Coordinate across functions
Material recovery cases often require commercial, pricing, planning, warehouse, and store coordination.

### 4.6 Preserve traceability
Major recovery decisions, markdown changes, transfer actions, replenishment reductions, and escalations must be documented appropriately.

---

## 5. Typical Causes of Post-Promotion Recovery Risk

Post-promotion recovery risk may arise from:

- campaign demand below plan
- forecast overestimation of uplift
- store execution weakness during promotion
- pricing or signage inconsistency
- over-positioned stock before campaign
- late campaign response correction
- future inbound not reduced in time
- store concentration of residual stock
- warehouse buffering above actual need
- regional demand variation
- weak product relevance after the campaign ends
- repeated campaign design weakness
- delayed markdown or transfer decisions
- replenishment not reduced quickly enough after event close

The actual cause should be validated before major corrective action is taken.

---

## 6. Common Recovery Categories

### 6.1 Store residual stock recovery
One or more stores retain materially high campaign stock after event close.

### 6.2 Regional residual stock recovery
A full region or cluster of stores holds elevated post-promotion inventory.

### 6.3 Warehouse campaign residual recovery
Warehouse stock positioned for campaign demand remains above healthy post-event need.

### 6.4 Inbound-linked recovery risk
Confirmed future inbound will worsen already elevated post-promotion stock.

### 6.5 Markdown-supported recovery
Inventory recovery likely requires controlled markdown or secondary offer action.

### 6.6 Transfer-supported recovery
Residual inventory can potentially be rebalanced toward healthier-demand locations.

### 6.7 Structural repeat recovery issue
Repeated promotions leave the same type of inventory overhang and may require structural campaign review.

---

## 7. Trigger Conditions

A post-promotion inventory case should be treated as a formal recovery response when one or more of the following apply:

- residual stock exceeds local recovery threshold after or near campaign end
- projected post-event cover is materially above target
- future inbound will worsen already elevated residual stock
- high-value or high-volume campaign item is affected
- warehouse capacity or storage efficiency is at risk
- multiple stores or a region are affected
- markdown, transfer, or replenishment change may be required
- local action alone is insufficient
- finance, pricing, or executive review may be required
- repeated residual stock pattern suggests structural campaign weakness

---

## 8. Required Assessment Fields

Every material post-promotion inventory recovery review should capture, where available:

- promotion ID or campaign reference
- SKU / item ID and description
- affected store, warehouse, or region scope
- promotion end date or expected close date
- current on-hand and usable inventory
- projected post-promotion days of cover
- expected normal post-event demand
- next confirmed inbound or transfer timing
- recovery category
- likely cause
- aging or slow-movement indicator where applicable
- current mitigation options
- priority classification
- response owner
- escalation status
- next review time
- final resolution and outcome

---

## 9. Standard Recovery Workflow

The normal post-promotion inventory recovery workflow should follow this sequence:

1. detect the residual inventory signal
2. validate campaign, stock, demand, and inbound facts
3. classify the recovery type and urgency
4. assess business impact and affected scope
5. identify feasible recovery options
6. prioritize the most material exposure
7. assign response owner
8. communicate to impacted teams
9. escalate if local action is insufficient
10. document final action, decision, and closure

The response should focus on reducing excess efficiently while protecting commercial control and inventory productivity.

---

## 10. Severity Levels

### Level 1 — Local manageable recovery
Residual stock exists but can be handled through normal local adjustment.

Examples:
- isolated store residual stock with moderate value
- one low-priority campaign item with manageable cover
- small post-event replenishment reduction opportunity

### Level 2 — Managed operational recovery
Recovery affects important inventory decisions and requires broader visibility.

Examples:
- moderate residual stock on important SKU
- selected regional post-campaign excess
- future inbound likely to worsen recovery position
- controlled markdown review may be needed

### Level 3 — Cross-functional recovery risk
Residual stock materially affects multiple locations, warehouse flow, or inventory value exposure and requires coordinated action.

Examples:
- regional campaign stock overhang
- warehouse residual stock linked to weak sell-through
- markdown, transfer, and replenishment adjustment needed together
- repeated post-promotion excess on important campaign item

### Level 4 — Executive or governance escalation
Recovery case creates major financial, operational, or strategic exposure and requires exceptional action or approval.

Examples:
- strategic campaign leaves major stock overhang across regions
- severe warehouse pressure from campaign residual stock
- large-value markdown or policy exception decision required
- repeated high-impact recovery failures requiring senior review

---

## 11. Business Impact Assessment

Post-promotion recovery cases should be assessed across the following areas.

### 11.1 Working capital impact
Will residual inventory tie up material cash or reduce inventory productivity?

### 11.2 Warehouse and flow impact
Will excess campaign stock create congestion or reduce handling efficiency?

### 11.3 Commercial recovery impact
Will markdown, alternate offer, or secondary support be needed?

### 11.4 Inventory quality impact
Will aging, expiry, or obsolescence risk increase if recovery is delayed?

### 11.5 Governance impact
Does the case require exception approval, executive review, or formal decision logging?

---

## 12. Immediate Recovery Options

Depending on the situation, teams may consider:

- reducing or pausing short-term replenishment
- delaying or reshaping future inbound where feasible
- reviewing inter-location transfer options
- initiating controlled markdown or secondary promotion review where approved
- prioritizing stock movement toward healthier demand locations
- increasing store-level sell-through focus on affected items
- protecting warehouse capacity by limiting further inflow
- reviewing alternate channel or outlet options where approved
- increasing monitoring frequency until residual stock normalizes
- escalating for controlled policy exception or executive approval

Any material deviation from normal policy must follow approved governance and approval paths.

---

## 13. Prioritization Rules During Recovery

When recovery capacity is limited, prioritization should generally favor:

1. high-value or high-volume residual stock
2. stock with aging, expiry, or obsolescence risk
3. inventory creating warehouse or flow pressure
4. campaign items with narrowing recovery window
5. lower-risk residual stock after higher-exposure inventory is addressed

Priority decisions should be documented when they materially change standard replenishment, transfer, markdown, or commercial behavior.

---

## 14. Communication Standards

When communicating a post-promotion inventory recovery case, the message should include:

- what campaign or item is affected
- affected stores, warehouse, or region
- current residual stock position
- likely cause
- expected business impact
- actions already taken
- action or decision now needed
- owner
- next update time

Avoid vague messages such as:

- “promo stock remains”
- “inventory is still high”
- “campaign stock is not moving”
- “please review recovery urgently”

Good communication must be clear, specific, and action-oriented.

---

## 15. Escalation Guidance

Escalation should be considered when:

- local recovery action is not enough
- multiple stores or a region are affected
- confirmed inbound will materially worsen the recovery case
- warehouse pressure or aging risk is becoming serious
- markdown, transfer, or allocation changes require management approval
- finance or commercial leadership alignment is needed
- repeated post-promotion residual patterns suggest structural weakness
- executive approval is needed for exception action

Escalation messages should state:

- issue summary
- affected business scope
- current timing and severity
- actions already taken
- decision or support required
- owner and timing

---

## 16. Investigation Guidance by Scenario

### 16.1 Store residual stock
Check:
- store sell-through after campaign close
- local execution quality during the campaign
- whether the stock can recover through normal demand
- need for transfer or markdown support
- whether replenishment is still flowing unnecessarily

### 16.2 Regional residual stock
Check:
- whether the issue is broad or concentrated
- regional demand pattern after campaign end
- whether stock can be rebalanced to healthier stores
- whether the issue reflects campaign design weakness

### 16.3 Warehouse campaign residual
Check:
- current warehouse stock exposure
- future inbound still in flow
- warehouse capacity effect
- whether stock should be redirected, delayed, or commercially recovered
- urgency of flow protection

### 16.4 Markdown-supported recovery
Check:
- expected markdown effectiveness
- margin trade-off
- pricing approval path
- remaining inventory depth
- whether markdown should be local, regional, or broader

### 16.5 Transfer-supported recovery
Check:
- source versus destination stock positions
- whether the transfer will solve the excess or only move it
- transport feasibility
- store demand health at receiving location
- timing of expected recovery

### 16.6 Structural repeat issue
Check:
- history of similar campaigns
- repeated item or category overhang
- forecast and positioning assumptions
- execution weakness pattern
- need for broader governance review

## 17. Link to EDIP Decisioning

Post-promotion inventory recovery matters to EDIP because it can directly affect:

- replenishment recommendations
- inbound adjustment decisions
- transfer suggestions
- overstock and aging risk interpretation
- planner override behavior
- markdown and commercial recovery planning
- executive exception decisioning

For this reason, material post-promotion recovery events should be visible to relevant commercial, planning, operations, and governance functions when they alter downstream decisions.

## 18. Data Quality and Traceability Expectations

Post-promotion inventory recovery records should meet these standards:

- valid campaign reference
- valid SKU and location scope
- usable timestamp
- current stock or cover reference
- known promotion end timing
- clear recovery classification
- owner identity
- next review time for active case
- mitigation or escalation note
- final resolution and learning captured for material cases

Poor-quality recovery records reduce learning quality and weaken inventory decision support.

## 19. Example Response Messages

### Example A — Store residual stock
**Subject:** Immediate review required for post-promotion residual stock at priority stores

**Message:**  
Stores 118 and 122 retain materially elevated stock for SKU 44219 following the close of the weekend campaign. Current sell-through is now below the level needed to normalize inventory within the expected post-event window. Planning should review short-term replenishment reduction immediately, and regional operations should confirm whether local transfer or commercial recovery support is needed by 11:30 AM today.

### Example B — Regional recovery case
**Subject:** Regional post-promotion inventory recovery review required

**Message:**  
North region now shows elevated post-campaign residual stock across three snack SKUs after promotional uplift finished below plan. Additional inbound remains scheduled and may worsen the current recovery position if not reviewed today. Commercial planning, supply chain planning, and regional operations coordination is required to determine recovery actions before excess exposure increases.

### Example C — Warehouse residual pressure
**Subject:** Warehouse campaign residual stock creating recovery risk

**Message:**  
Warehouse W03 continues to hold above-threshold residual inventory for a promoted beverage SKU family after campaign close. The current stock position is beginning to reduce storage efficiency, and standard outbound flow is not recovering inventory fast enough. Warehouse operations, planning, and commercial teams should review inbound adjustment and secondary recovery options before 2:00 PM today.

### Example D — Executive escalation
**Subject:** Executive approval required for major post-promotion inventory recovery action

**Message:**  
A strategic campaign has ended with major residual stock exposure across two regions, and standard recovery actions are unlikely to normalize inventory without broader intervention. Planning recommends temporary controlled action across replenishment reduction, stock rebalancing, and commercial recovery pathways. Executive direction is required before 4:00 PM today.

---

## 20. Non-Compliant Response Examples

The following are poor practices:

- waiting too long after campaign close to start recovery review
- continuing normal replenishment despite visible residual stock exposure
- allowing confirmed inbound to worsen avoidable post-promotion excess
- changing markdown or transfer behavior without documented reasoning
- failing to inform impacted teams about material recovery risk
- using vague “promo stock left” messages without timing or scope
- closing the case without capturing learning for future campaigns
