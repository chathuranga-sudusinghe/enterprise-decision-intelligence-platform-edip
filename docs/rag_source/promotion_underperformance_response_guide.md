## 1. Purpose

This guide defines the standard response approach for promotion underperformance situations at NorthStar Retail & Distribution (NRD).

Its purpose is to ensure that when a promotion performs below expected demand, sell-through, or commercial impact levels, the business responds in a controlled, timely, and business-focused way before the issue creates avoidable overstock, poor campaign ROI, pricing inefficiency, warehouse pressure, or repeated execution waste.

This guide supports the Enterprise Decision Intelligence Platform (EDIP) by providing governed knowledge for:

- promotion underperformance detection
- campaign recovery review
- residual inventory risk handling
- pricing and execution follow-up
- replenishment and inbound adjustment
- escalation and approval support
- audit-aware decision traceability

---

## 2. Scope

This guide applies to promotion underperformance situations involving:

- lower-than-expected campaign demand
- weak sell-through during active promotion
- poor uplift versus forecast or plan
- store execution weakness affecting campaign results
- pricing or signage issues reducing response
- campaign inventory remaining above expected residual level
- regional campaign underperformance
- post-promotion excess stock exposure
- repeated weak campaign response on similar item groups
- promotion conditions that materially weaken commercial return or inventory efficiency

This guide applies to teams such as:

- commercial planning
- merchandising
- pricing teams
- supply chain planning
- demand planning
- regional operations
- store operations
- warehouse operations
- inventory control
- finance support where campaign return or inventory value exposure is relevant
- executive stakeholders for major commercial risk cases

## 3. Definition of Promotion Underperformance

A promotion underperformance exists when a planned or active promotion delivers materially weaker demand response, sell-through, customer take-up, or business impact than expected within the relevant review window.

Examples include:

- actual uplift is materially below promotional forecast
- store sell-through remains weak despite promotional pricing
- campaign stock remains high during or after the event
- display or pricing execution fails to support expected response
- regional promotional response is materially below target
- expected traffic or conversion benefit does not materialize
- repeated campaigns on similar products underperform in a similar pattern

A promotion underperformance is not only a marketing issue.  
It is a commercial, inventory, and operational efficiency risk.

---

## 4. Core Principles

All promotion underperformance responses must follow these principles.

### 4.1 Detect early
Weak campaign performance should be identified during the active response window whenever possible, not only after the campaign ends.

### 4.2 Focus on business impact
Response decisions must consider sales recovery, inventory exposure, pricing efficiency, execution quality, and campaign ROI.

### 4.3 Separate execution failure from true weak demand
The business should distinguish between low consumer response and poor campaign execution.

### 4.4 Protect inventory flow and commercial discipline
When a promotion underperforms, the response should reduce excess exposure without creating uncontrolled reactive behavior.

### 4.5 Coordinate across functions
Material promotion underperformance often requires commercial, pricing, planning, store, and warehouse alignment.

### 4.6 Preserve traceability
Major campaign recovery decisions, pricing changes, replenishment changes, markdowns, and escalations must be documented appropriately.

## 5. Typical Causes of Promotion Underperformance

Promotion underperformance may arise from:

- weak customer demand response
- poor campaign design
- insufficient discount attractiveness
- pricing inconsistency
- missing or weak in-store execution
- missing display materials
- poor timing of campaign launch
- wrong item selection
- forecast overestimation of uplift
- local demand misread
- competitor activity
- stock placed in the wrong locations
- campaign communication weakness
- store execution inconsistency across regions
- product quality or assortment relevance issue

The actual cause should be validated before major corrective action is taken.

---

## 6. Common Promotion Underperformance Categories

### 6.1 Demand-response underperformance
Customers respond below expected promotional uplift despite acceptable execution.

### 6.2 Execution-linked underperformance
Campaign performance is weakened primarily by store, pricing, signage, or display execution issues.

### 6.3 Pricing-linked underperformance
Discount or price architecture is not strong enough, not clear enough, or not correctly reflected in store.

### 6.4 Regional underperformance
The campaign performs materially below plan in one region or store cluster.

### 6.5 Inventory residual underperformance
Campaign stock remains materially above expected levels during or after the promotion.

### 6.6 Forecast-linked underperformance
The campaign plan assumed stronger uplift than the market actually delivered.

### 6.7 Repeated campaign weakness
Similar promotion types, item groups, or mechanics repeatedly underperform and may require structural review.

---

## 7. Trigger Conditions

A promotion should be treated as a formal underperformance response case when one or more of the following apply:

- actual uplift is materially below planned range
- sell-through remains below threshold during the active campaign window
- campaign inventory remains above expected trajectory
- high-value, high-volume, or strategically important promotion is affected
- multiple stores or a region show the same weak response
- store execution issue is suspected
- post-promotion excess inventory risk is rising
- finance, pricing, or commercial review is needed
- local action alone is insufficient
- executive or policy exception may be required

## 8. Required Assessment Fields

Every material promotion underperformance review should capture, where available:

- promotion ID or campaign reference
- SKU / item ID and description
- affected store, region, or channel scope
- promotion start date and end date
- planned uplift or target performance
- actual demand or sell-through performance
- underperformance category
- likely cause
- current on-hand and usable inventory
- projected residual inventory after campaign
- pricing and execution status
- mitigation options considered
- priority classification
- response owner
- escalation status
- next review time
- final resolution and outcome

## 9. Standard Response Workflow

The normal promotion underperformance response workflow should follow this sequence:

1. detect the underperformance signal
2. validate demand, pricing, execution, and inventory facts
3. classify the issue type and urgency
4. assess business impact and affected scope
5. identify feasible recovery or containment options
6. prioritize the most material exposure
7. assign response owner
8. communicate to impacted teams
9. escalate if local action is insufficient
10. document final action, decision, and closure

The response should focus on improving commercial outcome where possible and containing inventory or financial exposure where recovery is limited.

## 10. Severity Levels

### Level 1 — Local manageable underperformance
Underperformance exists but can be handled through local correction or monitoring.

Examples:
- isolated store execution issue
- low-priority campaign with limited inventory exposure
- moderate uplift miss with manageable recovery options

### Level 2 — Managed operational underperformance
Underperformance affects important campaign or inventory decisions and requires broader visibility.

Examples:
- moderate weak response on important SKU
- selected regional underperformance
- pricing or signage issue affecting multiple stores
- campaign residual stock beginning to rise

### Level 3 — Cross-functional campaign recovery risk
Underperformance materially affects commercial outcome, inventory exposure, or multiple locations and requires coordinated action.

Examples:
- major campaign with poor sell-through across region
- forecasted uplift materially not achieved
- store execution, pricing, and planning all require intervention
- warehouse and replenishment plans need adjustment

### Level 4 — Executive or governance escalation
Underperformance creates major financial, operational, or strategic exposure and requires exceptional action or approval.

Examples:
- strategic campaign failing across multiple regions
- large-value campaign stock overhang
- material markdown, policy deviation, or commercial exception decision required
- repeated campaign design weakness requiring senior review

---

## 11. Business Impact Assessment

Promotion underperformance cases should be assessed across the following areas.

### 11.1 Sales impact
Is the campaign failing to generate the expected revenue or traffic response?

### 11.2 Inventory impact
Will the weak response leave excess stock, campaign residuals, or warehouse pressure?

### 11.3 Pricing efficiency impact
Is the current discount structure ineffective or commercially misaligned?

### 11.4 Operational impact
Will stores, warehouses, planners, or pricing teams need to change current activity?

### 11.5 Governance impact
Does the case require exception approval, executive review, or formal decision logging?

---

## 12. Immediate Mitigation Options

Depending on the situation, teams may consider:

- validating pricing and signage execution immediately
- correcting display or in-store placement issues
- reviewing whether discount structure should be adjusted where approved
- reducing or pausing future replenishment
- delaying or reshaping future inbound where feasible
- reviewing inter-location transfer options for residual stock
- increasing markdown or secondary promotion review where approved
- increasing monitoring cadence during the remaining campaign window
- escalating for controlled commercial or replenishment exception approval
- flagging the campaign for urgent post-event learning review

Any material deviation from normal policy must follow approved governance and approval paths.

---

## 13. Prioritization Rules During Promotion Underperformance

When response capacity is limited, prioritization should generally favor:

1. high-value or high-volume promotions
2. campaigns with rising excess inventory exposure
3. strategically important or high-visibility promotions
4. promotions still within a recovery window
5. lower-risk underperforming campaigns after higher-exposure cases are addressed

Priority decisions should be documented when they materially change standard pricing, replenishment, or campaign support behavior.

---

## 14. Communication Standards

When communicating a promotion underperformance case, the message should include:

- what campaign or item is affected
- affected stores, region, or channel
- expected versus actual performance
- likely cause
- current inventory or residual stock exposure
- actions already taken
- action or decision now needed
- owner
- next update time

Avoid vague messages such as:

- “the promotion is weak”
- “sales are not good”
- “campaign is slow”
- “please check promo performance”

Good communication must be clear, specific, and action-oriented.

---

## 15. Escalation Guidance

Escalation should be considered when:

- local recovery action is not enough
- multiple stores or regions are affected
- campaign residual inventory risk is rising materially
- pricing, planning, and store execution all require coordinated adjustment
- warehouse or working capital exposure is becoming meaningful
- finance or commercial leadership alignment is needed
- repeated weak campaign pattern suggests structural weakness
- executive approval is needed for exception action

Escalation messages should state:

- issue summary
- affected business scope
- current timing and severity
- actions already taken
- decision or support required
- owner and timing

## 16. Investigation Guidance by Scenario

### 16.1 Demand-response underperformance
Check:
- actual uplift versus planned range
- whether response is weak across all locations or only some
- item relevance to target shopper
- competitor activity
- whether the campaign mechanic is attractive enough

### 16.2 Execution-linked underperformance
Check:
- display setup completion
- signage accuracy
- shelf placement and visibility
- price tag correctness
- store-level compliance consistency
- whether late setup affected early demand window

### 16.3 Pricing-linked underperformance
Check:
- approved price versus executed price
- discount competitiveness
- customer value perception
- price communication clarity
- whether deeper discount or alternate mechanic review is needed

### 16.4 Regional underperformance
Check:
- region-specific response pattern
- local execution differences
- regional demand conditions
- whether issue is isolated or broadly repeated
- need for targeted regional correction

### 16.5 Inventory residual underperformance
Check:
- remaining campaign days
- projected end-of-campaign residual stock
- replenishment still in flow
- warehouse capacity effect
- markdown, transfer, or post-event recovery options

### 16.6 Repeated campaign weakness
Check:
- history of similar campaign type
- repeated item or category pattern
- forecast accuracy history
- commercial design assumptions
- need for structural campaign review

## 17. Link to EDIP Decisioning

Promotion underperformance matters to EDIP because it can directly affect:

- replenishment recommendations
- inbound adjustment decisions
- transfer suggestions
- overstock and residual inventory risk interpretation
- planner override behavior
- markdown and recovery planning
- executive exception decisioning

For this reason, material promotion underperformance events should be visible to relevant commercial, planning, operations, and governance functions when they alter downstream decisions.

## 18. Data Quality and Traceability Expectations

Promotion underperformance records should meet these standards:

- valid campaign reference
- valid SKU and location scope
- usable timestamp
- planned versus actual performance reference
- current inventory or residual exposure reference
- clear issue classification
- owner identity

## 19. Example Response Messages

### Example A — Execution-linked issue
**Subject:** Immediate review required for weak campaign response in priority stores

**Message:**  
The approved weekend beverage promotion is currently performing below expected uplift in Stores 118 and 122. Initial review suggests possible execution weakness, including incomplete display visibility and inconsistent signage. Store operations and merchandising should validate execution status immediately, and commercial planning should reassess the expected recovery window by 11:30 AM today.

### Example B — Regional weak response
**Subject:** Regional underperformance emerging for featured snack campaign

**Message:**  
North region shows materially weaker sell-through than planned for the active featured snack campaign across six stores. Current demand is below forecasted promotional range, and projected residual inventory after campaign close is increasing. Commercial planning, regional operations, and supply chain planning coordination is required today to review recovery and containment options.

### Example C — Residual inventory risk
**Subject:** Campaign residual stock exposure requires response review

**Message:**  
A promoted household SKU remains materially above the expected stock trajectory with three campaign days remaining. If the current pace continues, the campaign will end with significant residual inventory in West region stores and additional inbound still planned. Planning and commercial teams should review replenishment reduction and post-campaign recovery options before 2:00 PM today.

### Example D — Executive escalation
**Subject:** Executive approval required for major promotion underperformance response

**Message:**  
A strategic campaign is now underperforming across two regions, with weak uplift and rising residual stock exposure. Current performance is materially below plan, and standard recovery actions may not be sufficient to contain commercial and inventory risk. Commercial planning recommends controlled corrective action across pricing, replenishment, and post-event recovery pathways. Executive direction is required before 4:00 PM today.

---

## 20. Non-Compliant Response Examples

The following are poor practices:

- waiting until campaign end before reacting to clear weak performance
- assuming weak demand is the only cause without checking execution quality
- continuing normal replenishment despite visible underperformance and rising residual risk
- changing commercial or pricing behavior without documented reasoning
- failing to inform impacted teams about material campaign weakness
- using vague “promo is slow” messages without performance context
- closing the case without capturing campaign learning
