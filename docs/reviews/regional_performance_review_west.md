# Regional Performance Review — West
## NorthStar Retail & Distribution
## File: docs/reviews/regional_performance_review_west.md

## 1. Purpose

This document reviews operational and demand-related business performance for the West region of NorthStar Retail & Distribution (NRD).

Its purpose is to provide a structured regional view of demand behavior, inventory exposure, replenishment reliability, supplier sensitivity, and service-level risk. This review supports regional operations, planning coordination, commercial interpretation, and grounded enterprise decision support within EDIP.

---

## 2. Review objectives

The objectives of this review are to:

- assess the business performance of the West region
- identify major regional demand and inventory patterns
- review whether replenishment support was sufficient
- highlight service-level and operational risk areas
- support targeted intervention where regional conditions differ from enterprise averages
- provide grounded regional context for RAG-supported explanation and decisioning

---

## 3. Scope

This review applies to:

- West region business performance during the review period
- regional demand behavior
- inventory and replenishment risk patterns
- supplier and inbound sensitivity affecting West operations
- regional service-level protection and execution consistency

This review does not cover:

- full enterprise-wide comparison in detail
- supplier contract negotiation decisions
- customer-level behavioral analysis
- non-merchandise internal-use operations

---

## 4. Executive summary

The West region delivered mixed but manageable overall performance. Core baseline demand remained healthy across much of the region, but operational performance was not uniformly stable. A subset of SKUs and locations showed a more sensitive combination of demand strength, replenishment timing pressure, and inbound uncertainty than seen in more stable areas.

The most important business observation is that West did not show broad structural failure, but it did show a higher concentration of cases where regional execution discipline mattered more than enterprise averages alone would suggest.

The main regional risk themes were:

- selected strong-demand items with lower stock coverage
- uneven replenishment recovery timing
- localized supplier-related vulnerability
- higher sensitivity to receiving or inbound delay
- pockets of service risk requiring closer planner attention

West should therefore be treated as a region requiring targeted, operationally aware review rather than simple aggregate planning assumptions.

---

## 5. High-level regional performance overview

### 5.1 Demand health
Demand in the West region was broadly solid, with several item groups showing resilient movement and acceptable customer pull.

### 5.2 Stability profile
While much of the region remained stable, performance was less even in certain locations and categories, especially where supply recovery timing weakened.

### 5.3 Risk concentration
The region’s main issue was not weak demand overall, but the concentration of risk in selected inventory and replenishment-sensitive cases.

### 5.4 Business interpretation
West is operationally viable and commercially active, but it requires stronger exception monitoring than more stable regions.

---

## 6. Demand observations in West

### 6.1 Baseline demand pattern
A large share of baseline demand in West remained within expected range, supporting normal commercial activity and replenishment interpretation.

### 6.2 Strong-mover concentration
A number of SKUs in the region showed stronger movement than average, which increased pressure on stock coverage when replenishment timing was not equally strong.

### 6.3 Localized volatility
Some stores or item clusters showed more variable sell-through than enterprise averages alone would suggest. These cases require targeted review rather than broad assumptions.

### 6.4 Interpretation caution
Not all volatility in West should be treated as structural demand change. Some cases may reflect promotion timing, pricing effects, local events, or stock-availability distortion.

---

## 7. Inventory and coverage review

### 7.1 Coverage pressure
The West region showed a meaningful number of cases where inventory coverage narrowed faster than desired on strong-moving items.

### 7.2 Availability exposure
Where demand stayed healthy and inbound timing weakened, service risk increased more quickly than standard-cycle assumptions would normally allow.

### 7.3 Balanced-stock zones
Some parts of the region maintained acceptable inventory balance and did not require unusual intervention.

### 7.4 Overstock pockets
A smaller subset of slower-moving items showed signs of elevated stock relative to current movement, indicating a need for more selective replenishment discipline or commercial review.

---

## 8. Replenishment performance interpretation

### 8.1 General replenishment support
Most replenishment activity remained directionally usable, but not all actions recovered stock quickly enough in the most sensitive West cases.

### 8.2 Recovery timing issue
The region showed a pattern where the problem was not always absence of replenishment action, but delay in recovery effectiveness.

### 8.3 Priority review need
Cases involving strong movers, lower coverage, and unstable inbound protection should be prioritized earlier in West than in lower-risk regions.

### 8.4 Planner implication
Planners should treat West as a region where recommendation review may require more operational caution and faster exception response.

---

## 9. Supplier and inbound sensitivity

### 9.1 Supplier-linked exposure
West appeared more exposed to supplier inconsistency in selected product flows, especially where inbound timing reliability was already weak.

### 9.2 Receiving dependency
The region’s service stability depends heavily on reliable receiving and timely inventory availability after inbound arrival.

### 9.3 Open-inbound caution
Open purchase orders should not automatically be treated as full protection for West where supplier or receiving reliability is under pressure.

### 9.4 Business implication
Supplier and inbound performance must be considered directly when interpreting West stock risk and replenishment urgency.

---

## 10. Service-level risk observations

### 10.1 Local service sensitivity
West showed a meaningful number of local service-risk cases even when enterprise-level demand signals looked manageable.

### 10.2 Critical item exposure
Critical or strong-moving SKUs were more vulnerable when coverage and inbound timing weakened at the same time.

### 10.3 Uneven operational pressure
Not all stores or clusters experienced the same level of service pressure. Regional targeting is therefore more useful than uniform response.

### 10.4 Escalation relevance
The most severe West cases may require faster planner escalation, especially where supplier instability, low coverage, and critical demand converge.

---

## 11. Commercial and pricing interpretation

### 11.1 Price-response variation
Selected items in West may have shown stronger or weaker price-response sensitivity than enterprise average, suggesting that commercial effects should be interpreted locally where needed.

### 11.2 Promotion sensitivity
Where promotion activity existed, the region may have experienced carryover or localized uplift that changed short-term replenishment pressure.

### 11.3 Caution on demand reading
Observed movement should be interpreted together with price and promotion context so that planners do not overreact to temporary uplift or underreact to real local strength.

---

## 12. Operational themes observed in West

The following operational themes were most relevant in the region:

- strong-demand SKUs with tighter stock coverage
- uneven replenishment recovery timing
- supplier and inbound sensitivity
- localized store-level or cluster-level variation
- moderate service pressure in selected cases
- isolated overstock on slower items
- need for earlier exception identification on vulnerable flows

These themes justify continued West-specific review.

---

## 13. Risk interpretation framework for West

West regional cases should be interpreted with greater caution when the following conditions appear together:

- strong current demand
- low or narrowing coverage days
- unstable inbound timing
- supplier inconsistency
- critical SKU status
- promotion-linked volatility
- weak substitute availability

When several of these conditions appear at once, the case should move into higher planner attention even if enterprise averages do not yet appear severe.

---

## 14. Recommended actions

The following actions are recommended for the West region:

- review strong-demand, low-coverage SKUs more frequently
- apply extra caution where open inbound supply is unreliable
- prioritize receiving discipline for West-sensitive product flows
- monitor supplier-linked risk on regionally important items
- slow or review replenishment for softer-demand, higher-stock items
- use regional context when interpreting price and promotion effects
- escalate severe service-risk combinations earlier when local conditions warrant it
